DeepEnsemble: A Novel Brain Wave Classification
in MI-BCI using Ensemble of Deep Learners

The project was undertaken during the first year of my Master's studies (2022-2023) and was supported by the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council (NSERC) of Canada. It was a collaborative effort with Arshad Mehtiyev (M.A.), with Prof. Marzieh Amini and Prof. Hamidreza Sadreazami providing supervision.

The project included a development of a DeepEnsemble model, an innovative deep learning-based solution, to classify EEG signals for right hand and right leg movements in a Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) system. Integrating diverse Deep Learning models like Transformers, MLP, CNN, XGboost and Hybrid models through an ensemble approach for enhanced accuracy.
We were able to outperform state-of-the-art methods, culminating in presentation and publication at the IEEE 41st International Conference on Consumer Electronics (2023 ICCE).

  • Full Paper

Project Abstract

Brain computer interfaces (BCIs) are rapidly growing in neurorehabilitation to realize communication for consumers with disabilities. This work proposes a classification approach for the motor imagery BCI electroencephalogram (EEG) signals using the recent advances in deep learning methods. The proposed DeepEnsemble method combines different deep learning models in an ensemble learning with soft voting. More specifically, multi-layer perceptron, vision transformer, convolutional neural network and its integration with distributed gradient boosting methods are explored separately and combined in an ensemble learning model. The proposed method is evaluated on a publicly available dataset. It is shown that the proposed ensemble learning method significantly improves the classification accuracy of the EEG signals across various subjects as compared to some of the existing methods.

Classifying Canadian Citizens’Financial Well-Being
Status and Predicting the Impact of Global Shocks

The project was undertaken during the winter of 2023 as part of my data science graduate studies course. It was a collaborative effort involving Zakaria Zoundi (PhD in Economics) and Huiqian Chen (M.A.), with Prof. Ahmed El-Roby providing supervision. The project received support from data provided by the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC).
This project comprises both a paper and a poster, both of which are available for review. The paper can be accessed via this link: FWB Report Paper. Additionally, you can view the poster in PDF format by clicking here: Financial Well-Being Poster. The poster was also presented at the Carleton University Institute for Data Science Data Day 9.0 in 2023.

  • Project Poster
  • Project full report

Project Abstract

This project utilizes FWB data from both pre- and post-COVID-19 surveys conducted by the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) to develop a machine learning model that can predict Canadians' FWB status and the potential impact of future global shocks. The project aims to investigate whether the key factors contributing to FWB change when ignoring COVID-19, and whether the same set of features can accurately predict FWB in both pre- and post-COVID-19 datasets. The results show that FWB is disproportionately distributed across the nation. The key drivers of FWB include: household financial situation, capacity to meet monthly expenses, saving, credit score. Besides, the analysis showed that the prediction of FWB has a higher level of accuracy when global shocks are not accounted for. This showed the significant disruptive effects the pandemic induced across sectors and individuals. The project advocates for an increasing effort from the government to help Canadians across provinces to better cope with their finances. Addressing this issue involves creating better jobs, a review of minimum wages, controlling inflation, supporting debt relief including mortgage, and further assistance to households in the lowest income deciles, among others. Particular attention should be given to groups that are usually left-behind such as women and single mothers, persons living with disability and indigenous communities.

DETECTING RISKY VEGETATION ENCROAHMENT ON
POWERLINES VIA MOBILE AND AIRBORNE LASER SCANNED
POINT CLOUDS

During my second year of Master's studies (2022-2023), I successfully completed a one-year project that received support through a research contract from Natural Resources Canada (NRCan). The main objective was to predict powerline failures using LiDAR data, which drove the development of an innovative deep learning-based solution for encroachment detection.

Conducted under the guidance and supervision of Professors Marzieh Amini, James Green, and Sareerman Rajan, the project involved thorough data preprocessing and cleaning. I employed an array of technologies including Python (ArcGIS, Keras, PyTorch), C programming, and Cloud Compare to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the data.

One of the notable contributions was the implementation of a novel point-based encroachment detection algorithm. This algorithm exhibited exceptional efficiency and achieved an impressive precision rate of 98% in identifying encroachments across diverse datasets obtained from different locations such as Surrey, Toronto, and Ottawa. This outcome demonstrates the robustness and effectiveness of the solution we developed.

Furthermore, the culmination of this project led to the creation of a journal article that has been submitted to the IEEE Sensors Journal for publication. Once the paper achieves acceptance, I will promptly share the link to provide access to the findings and insights derived from this research endeavor.

Securing the IoT Landscape: Threats, Mitigations,
and Emerging Technologies

The project was undertaken during the second year of my Master's studies (2022-2023) during the introduction to Internet of Things course with Prof. Mohamed Ibn Kahla. The project was a collaborative effort with Iman Alavi Fazel (M.A.), Mahmoud Selim (M.A.), Shakir Sayed (M.A.), with Prof. Mohamed Ibn Kahla providing supervision.

This project is about examining the security challenges and solutions within the Internet of Things (IoT) industry in 2023, given its significant growth and increasing vulnerability to security breaches. It delves into the unique security landscape of IoT, analyzes various security attacks and their mitigations across different layers of IoT infrastructure, and discusses emerging technologies like edge computing and blockchain for enhancing security. The project also includes case studies of notable security incidents, aiming to provide a comprehensive resource for practitioners and developers in the field to better understand and address IoT security concerns.

The project comprises both a full project report with all findings and solutions, and weekly update presentation slides, all of wich are available for review.

  • View Project Report
  • View Slides

Project Abstract

The Internet of Things (IoT) industry has expanded significantly in recent years by adopting new technologies and integrating with existing ones. With this expansion, the vulnerability to security breaches has escalated, leading to greater financial implications and posing a direct risk to individuals' well-being. In this project, we summarized the security aspects of these systems and discussed the solutions proposed in the literature. We first explored what is called “security landscape”, which refers to the unique characteristics of IoT and the security implications they have. Subsequently, different security attacks and their mitigations have been presented. These threats can target various aspects of an IoT infrastructure, from the sensor devices and actuators to the cloud and backend services. To provide this information in an organized manner, different threats were analyzed relative to the 4-layer model of IoT. After examining different attack surfaces and their prevention mechanisms, the use of edge computing and blockchain-based technologies as emerging technologies were discussed. As case studies, the last section of this report contains information about some of the previous well-known security incidents that occurred in the field. These details include the security flaws that caused these security attacks, the damages they imposed, and the lessons learned from them. This report can be useful for any practitioners and developers in the field and can provide a generalized overview of the challenges and possible solutions.

Lane Detection and Object Detection algorithms
for Self-Driving RC Car

This project was undertaken during the academic year of 2020-2021 as part of my undergraduate thesis. It was a collaborative effort involving Omar Ammar (BSc in Electrical and Electronics Engineering) and Mustafa Almoghrabi (M.A. in Telecomunication Engineering), with Prof. Cem Direkoğlu providing supervision.
This project comprises both a short report, a full report, and a Project Demonstration video, all are available for review. The full report can be accessed via this link: Auto RC report. Additionally, you can view the demonstrattion video on YouTube by clicking here: Auto RC Demo.

Project Introduction

The existence of cars started to become dangerous in man-kind life, especially with the massive increase in cars. This increase reached the extent that 1.35 million deaths worldwide due to vehicle crashes [1]. This considerable number of accidents is mainly due to human error and inattention. From here, the idea of self-driving cars came as a good assistant in improving road safety and offering new mobility options for millions of people, especially old-aged and people with low vision. The proposed self-driving car guides itself using visual information from its environment with an HD camera. The data obtained from the camera are sent directly to the Raspberry pi that plays a significant role in sending control signals to different parts of the car. It is worth mentioning that the proposed car will be existent with two modes: Manual with the help of a warning system supported with machine vision algorithms to detect all kinds of obstacles, and autonomous as the car will drive itself and take decisions at the right time. Many companies started to invest in these self-driving cars like "Waymo," which began in 2016 in developing, and in 2020 they started self driving taxi service [2]. The following project can be another step towards more complex and more significant scale projects. It is considered relatively cheap with a cost of 1400 TL. This final design report will explain the design and algorithms used in a self-driving car and describe the subsystems' functionality inside it. It will also explain the concepts used in image processing and control systems, in addition to the results of the tests conducted until reaching the final product. This report also gives user manual explaining in detail how the car can be used, and at the end, some discussions about this project was presented.

Transforming Text: NLP and Transformers
Exploration

Welcome to the "Transforming Text: NLP and Transformers Exploration" project. This project is collection of notebooks I built that delves into the realm of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and its intersection with Transformers, offering an in-depth exploration of various techniques and applications.

The repository begins with a focus on NLP pre-processing, detailing fundamental techniques such as stopwords removal, tokenization, stemming, lemmatization, and Unicode normalization. Building on this foundation, the project delves into attention mechanisms in NLP, explaining concepts like encoder-decoder attention, self-attention, bidirectional attention, and multi-head attention.

Moving into practical applications, the repository covers sentiment analysis using flair and Transformers, named entity recognition with Spacy, open-domain question answering with Elasticsearch, and even extends to projects like analyzing sentiment and organizational entities from Reddit posts. Moreover, it explores similarity search techniques using Transformers and PyTorch. By combining theoretical explanations with hands-on implementation, "Transforming Text" serves as a valuable resource for those looking to understand the nuances of NLP and harness the potential of Transformers in real-world scenarios.
