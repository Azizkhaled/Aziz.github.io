DETECTING RISKY VEGETATION ENCROAHMENT ON POWERLINES VIA MOBILE AND AIRBORNE LASER SCANNED POINT CLOUDS

During my second year of Master's studies (2022-2023), I successfully completed a one-year project that received support through a research contract from Natural Resources Canada (NRCan). The main objective was to predict powerline failures using LiDAR data, which drove the development of an innovative deep learning-based solution for encroachment detection.
Conducted under the guidance and supervision of Professors Marzieh Amini, James Green, and Sreeraman Rajan, the project involved thorough data preprocessing and cleaning. I employed an array of technologies including Python (ArcGIS, Keras, PyTorch), C programming, and Cloud Compare to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the data.
One of the notable contributions was the implementation of a novel point-based encroachment detection algorithm. This algorithm exhibited exceptional efficiency and achieved an impressive precision rate of 98% in identifying encroachments across diverse datasets obtained from different locations such as Surrey, Toronto, and Ottawa. This outcome demonstrates the robustness and effectiveness of the solution we developed.
Furthermore, the culmination of this project led to the creation of a journal article that has been submitted to the IEEE Sensors Journal for publication. Once the paper achieves acceptance, I will promptly share the link to provide access to the findings and insights derived from this research endeavor.
